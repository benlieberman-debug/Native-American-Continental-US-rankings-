Major Tribes

1. Cherokee

The Cherokee became one of the most politically organized Native nations in U.S. history.

Despite forced removal during the Trail of Tears, the Cherokee rebuilt strong governments, schools, and modern economic systems.

2. Navajo

The Navajo Nation is the largest Native reservation in the United States.

Known for resilience, code talkers in World War II, and a strong cultural identity.

3. Sioux

The Sioux confederation became famous for military resistance against U.S. expansion.

Leaders like Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse remain legendary figures in Native history.

4. Apache

The Apache tribes were highly skilled warriors who resisted colonization for decades.

Geronimo became one of the most recognized Native resistance leaders in history.

5. Iroquois Confederacy

The Haudenosaunee Confederacy developed one of the most advanced political systems in pre-colonial North America.

Their influence may have inspired aspects of early American democratic thought.

6. Comanche

The Comanche dominated large parts of the Great Plains through horse warfare and mobility.

For a period, they controlled one of the most powerful Native territories in North America.
