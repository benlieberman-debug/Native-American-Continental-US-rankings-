Native American Tribe Rankings

Ranking major Native American tribes in the continental United States based on military strength, historical legacy, political influence, territorial control, and modern economic prosperity.

Continental U.S. Tribal Locations

Each colored region represents the historic territory of a major Native American tribe. Numbers indicate the final ranking.

Major Tribes

#1 Iroquois

The Iroquois Confederacy developed one of the most advanced political systems in pre-colonial North America.

Their influence shaped diplomacy, governance, and intertribal alliances across the Northeast.

#2 Sioux

The Sioux became legendary for military resistance against U.S. expansion across the Great Plains.

Leaders such as Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse remain iconic historical figures.

#3 Comanche

The Comanche dominated large parts of the Southern Plains through horse warfare and unmatched mobility.

For generations they controlled one of the strongest Native territories in North America.

#4 Navajo

The Navajo Nation is the largest Native reservation in the United States.

Known for resilience, cultural strength, and the Navajo Code Talkers during World War II.

#5 Apache

The Apache tribes were famous for guerrilla warfare and long resistance against colonization.

Geronimo became one of the most recognized Native resistance leaders in history.

#6 Cherokee

The Cherokee built one of the most politically organized Native societies in U.S. history.

Despite forced removal during the Trail of Tears, they rebuilt strong governments and economic systems.

#7 Seminole

The Seminole resisted U.S. military campaigns in Florida for decades.

They became known for resilience and successful resistance during the Seminole Wars.

#8 Chippewa

The Chippewa (Ojibwe) controlled major areas around the Great Lakes region.

They maintained powerful trade networks and strong cultural traditions.

#9 Choctaw

The Choctaw became known for diplomacy, adaptation, and rebuilding after removal.

They remain one of the largest and most influential Native nations today.
